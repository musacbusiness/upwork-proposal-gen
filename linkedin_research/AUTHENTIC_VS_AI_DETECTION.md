Notice: Different lengths. Different depth. Different learning points. **Feels lived.**

### LANGUAGE RED FLAGS

#### 1. **Corporate Formality**
**AI phrases:**
- "I'm pleased to announce..."
- "I'm delighted to share..."
- "It is critical that..."
- "It is important to note that..."
- "I am thrilled to present..."

**Why it's AI:** Real professionals don't talk this way. No human says "I am delighted." They say "I'm excited" or just "Here's a thing I'm sharing."

**Authentic equivalent:**
- "Wanted to share..."
- "So here's the thing..."
- "Got some good news..."
- "Let me be real with you..."

#### 2. **Lack of Contractions**
**Red flag (AI):**
> "I have learned that it is important to do this. You should not skip it."

**Authentic version:**
> "I've learned it's important to do this. You shouldn't skip it."

**Why:** Humans write with contractions 90% of the time in casual contexts. No contractions = robotic.

#### 3. **Generic Jargon Overload**
**AI loves these words:**
- "Leverage" (instead of "use")
- "Synergy" (instead of "working together")
- "Paradigm shift" (instead of "change")
- "Moving forward" (instead of "next")
- "At the end of the day" (filler)
- "It goes without saying" (then why say it?)
- "Needless to say" (same problem)

**Authentic:** Use the simple word. "Use this method." Not "leverage this methodology."

#### 4. **Every Sentence is Grammatically Perfect**
**Red flag pattern:**
Each sentence is well-formed, properly punctuated, no typos. Ever.

**Why it's AI:** Humans make small mistakes. Typos happen. Grammar slips. It's normal.

**Authentic pattern:** Occasional typo. Informal grammar ("gonna," "wanna"). Sentence fragments. Contractions. These imperfections build trust.

#### 5. **No Personality Words**
**Red flag (sterile):**
> "Professional growth requires consistent effort and strategic planning to achieve optimal results."

**Authentic (human):**
> "I realized I'd been spinning my wheels. Then I got serious about it. Actually worked."

**Why:** The authentic version has personality. Admits struggle. Uses simple words. Shows thinking progression.

### CONTENT RED FLAGS

#### 1. **No Personal Story, Just Facts**
**Red flag:**
> "LinkedIn has 900 million users. Daily active users number 260 million. Professionals use it to build networks. Research shows engagement rates have increased 23% this year."

**Why it's AI:** No experience. No "I." No stakes. Just recited information.

**Authentic:**
> "I started on LinkedIn thinking everyone was in suits talking about synergy. Then I actually built my network. Turns out the best conversations happen with real humans being honest."

**Why:** First-hand experience. Specific observation. Personal voice.

#### 2. **Vague Scenarios with Dropped Names**
**Red flag:**
> "I recently spoke with a Fortune 500 CEO who told me something interesting. He said that most professionals don't understand LinkedIn. I was shocked."

**Why it's AI:** Name-dropping for credibility but no actual context. Sounds made up.

**Authentic:**
> "Sarah, a SaaS founder I've worked with, admitted she'd been posting for 3 months with zero traction. We changed her approach. Suddenly, her posts got 1,000+ likes."

**Why:** Specific enough to believe (real detail). But privacy-conscious (first name only). Added context (what changed).

#### 3. **Generic Advice**
**Red flag:**
> "Always be authentic. Provide value. Engage with your community. Stay consistent. These are the keys to LinkedIn success."

**Why it's AI:** True but useless. Applies to everything. No specificity.

**Authentic:**
> "Here's what authenticity actually means: I admitted my failures. I disagreed with popular LinkedIn advice. My followers appreciated the honesty. Comments tripled."

**Why:** Shows what authenticity means. Provides proof. Specific.

#### 4. **No Nuance or Contradiction**
**Red flag:**
> "LinkedIn posting is essential for growth. You must post regularly. Consistency is key. Your network depends on it."

All agreement. All one direction. Simple.

**Authentic:**
> "I used to think posting daily was necessary. Now I post 2x per week and get better results. But consistency still matters‚ÄîI do it at the same times. It's weird how that works."

**Why:** Shows thinking evolved. Acknowledges complexity. Admits seeming contradiction.

#### 5. **No Admission of Failure**
**Red flag:**
> "I've always been successful on LinkedIn. My strategies work perfectly. Everyone should do what I do."

**Why it's AI:** Unrealistic. Everyone fails sometimes.

**Authentic:**
> "My first 10 posts flopped. Like, genuinely terrible. Then I realized I was trying to sound smart instead of being real. That shift changed everything."

**Why:** Honest. Relatable. Credible.

#### 6. **Equal Weight to All Ideas**
**Red flag:**
```
5 Things That Helped Me:
1. Posting more
2. Using better hashtags
3. Engaging with others
4. Optimizing my headline
5. Asking for help
```

All listed same. No priority. No emphasis.

**Authentic:**
> "Three things moved the needle: First, I stopped trying to sound professional. That alone changed everything. Second, I asked questions instead of giving advice. Third, yeah, consistency mattered. But honestly, the first two were 80% of it."

**Why:** Shows thinking. Hierarchy. What actually mattered vs. what was just 'one thing I did.'

#### 7. **No Personal Anecdotes**
**Red flag:**
> "LinkedIn is a powerful platform. Users benefit from consistent engagement. Companies see ROI from LinkedIn marketing. Professional development is important."

Literally no personal experience mentioned.

**Authentic:**
> "When I hit 10,000 followers, I expected something magical. Nope. Then I got a DM from someone who said my post about failing saved their job interview. That felt magical. That's why I keep posting."

**Why:** Personal moment. Real consequence. Emotional weight.

## SUBTLE RED FLAGS (Harder to Spot)

### Red Flag 1: **Boring Conclusions**
**AI ending:**
> "In conclusion, these strategies can help you succeed on LinkedIn. I hope you found this helpful. Please like and share."

**Authentic ending:**
> "So yeah, I was wrong about everything. Embrace that. Your weird takes will resonate more than polished advice."

**Why:** AI ends with summary + CTA. Humans end with realization or invitation to debate.

### Red Flag 2: **Formulaic Emotional Beats**
**AI emotion pattern:**
> "I was struggling. Then I found the answer. Now I'm thriving. You can too."

Exact same emotional arc every time. Perfect three-act structure.

**Authentic:** Messier. Sometimes you're still struggling. Sometimes the answer has complications. Sometimes you succeeded then failed again.

### Red Flag 3: **Too Many Hashtags**
**Red flag:**
> "Great article! #LinkedIn #Growth #Strategy #Personal Branding #Networking #Success #Business #Leadership"

Looks like hashtag spam.

**Authentic:**
> "Posted a thing about this today. #LinkedIn" (1-2 max)

### Red Flag 4: **Excessive Capitalization**
**Red flag:**
> "This CHANGED MY LIFE. It's INCREDIBLE. The RESULTS speak for themselves."

Artificial emphasis.

**Authentic:** Normal capitalization, maybe one word emphasized for actual emphasis.

### Red Flag 5: **Flowery Adjectives**
**Red flag phrases (AI loves these):**
- "amazing"
- "incredible"
- "phenomenal"
- "groundbreaking"
- "revolutionary"
- "transformational"
- "life-changing"

**Used 3+ times in one post = AI pattern.**

**Authentic:** Specific nouns instead. "I booked 12 calls" not "It's incredible."

## AUTHENTICITY SIGNALS: HOW TO IDENTIFY REAL POSTS

### Signal 1: **Specific Numbers (Not Rounded)**
**Authentic:**
- "I booked 72 meetings"
- "Lost 3 followers"
- "It took 8 months"
- "Posted on January 12, 2024"

**Why:** Real numbers are weird and specific. AI tends to round (50, 100, etc.).

### Signal 2: **Self-Contradiction**
**Authentic:**
> "I tell people not to post daily. But I actually post daily. I'm a hypocrite. Here's why."

Shows nuanced thinking. Real humans contradict themselves.

### Signal 3: **Admitted Mistakes**
**Authentic:**
> "I told you to do X for 2 years. I was wrong. Here's what actually works."

Shows learning and humility.

### Signal 4: **Specific Date/Timeline References**
**Authentic:**
- "When I launched in March..."
- "Last Tuesday, someone DMed me..."
- "Year 2 of doing this..."

**Why:** Real experience is grounded in time.

### Signal 5: **Conversational Voice**
**Authentic markers:**
- Fragments. ("So here's what happened.")
- Rhetorical questions. ("Ever notice how...?")
- Casual transitions. ("Anyway, point is...")
- Occasional typos or grammar slip
- Contractions everywhere

**Why:** How humans actually talk.

### Signal 6: **Disagreeable Takes**
**Authentic:**
> "I disagree with this popular advice..."
> "Most people hate this opinion, but..."
> "This will piss some people off, but..."

Posts willing to be wrong or controversial are usually real.

### Signal 7: **Author Engagement**
**Authentic signal:**
- Creator replies to comments within first 30 minutes
- Asks follow-up questions in replies
- Engages with other people's posts regularly
- Doesn't disappear after posting

**AI posts:** Often posted and abandoned. No interaction.

### Signal 8: **Visual Inconsistency**
**Authentic:**
- Different post formats throughout timeline
- Some short, some long
- Some with images, some without
- Not every post is perfectly structured
- Voice varies slightly post-to-post

**AI posts:** Often consistent template. Similar length. Similar structure.

### Signal 9: **Vulnerability**
**Authentic signals:**
- Admits failure
- Shares real struggles
- Says "I don't know"
- Shows learning journey
- Not afraid of seeming weak

### Signal 10: **Unique Perspective**
**Authentic:**
Posts that share something genuinely only they would know.

> "Everyone talks about LinkedIn engagement. But nobody mentions the weird feeling of watching your network grow from people you genuinely dislike. How do you deal with that?"

**Why:** Only someone with real LinkedIn experience would think of this specific weird thing.

## THE CHECKLIST: IS THIS POST AUTHENTIC?

Score 1 point for each authentic signal:

- [ ] Specific numbers (not rounded)
- [ ] Personal anecdote or story
- [ ] Admits a mistake or changes view
- [ ] Shows vulnerability
- [ ] Has typo(s) or grammatical imperfection
- [ ] Conversational tone (contractions, fragments)
- [ ] Takes a contrarian stance
- [ ] References specific timeline/date
- [ ] Author actively engages in comments
- [ ] Shows nuance or contradiction

**Score:**
- **8-10:** Almost certainly authentic
- **6-7:** Probably authentic
- **4-5:** Possibly AI-assisted (hybrid)
- **0-3:** Likely AI-generated

## THE PSYCHOLOGICAL PATTERN

**Why AI-generated content feels "off":**

1. **Perfect symmetry** - Real thoughts aren't perfectly balanced
2. **No risk** - Authentic posts take positions; AI hedges
3. **Lack of specificity** - AI generalizes; humans specify
4. **Emotional flatness** - AI doesn't have lived experience
5. **Absence of consequence** - AI lists ideas; humans tell stories with stakes
6. **No unique perspective** - AI recombines existing ideas

**Humans feel this. They don't always know why. But they know.**
